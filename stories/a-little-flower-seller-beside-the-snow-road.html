ARMENIA · 09/03/2024–10/03/2024

A Little Flower Seller Beside the Snow Road

After the first frightening climb into Armenia, the road softened for one night beside Lake Sevan. Then, on the way toward the Georgia border, a small figure standing alone in the snow made Tuzi pull over immediately.

On 9 March 2024, Toyotaki stayed near Hayravank Monastery, beside Lake Sevan. The monastery, the lake, the snow, and the open sky gave the journey a quiet pause after the hard first days in Armenia.

The next day, Tuzi continued toward the Georgia customs direction. On a snowy roadside, she saw a young girl standing alone. At first, the mind could not fully process the scene. Why was a child here? Where was her mother?

“Why you alone here? Where’s your mum mum?”

The child could only answer in her own language. Tuzi heard sounds like “mum mum,” but could not understand enough to know whether the girl was lost, waiting, working, or in trouble.

So Tuzi waved for help. A kind couple stopped their car. They spoke with the girl in a language Tuzi could not understand, but the meaning slowly became clear: the girl lived nearby. She was standing beside the highway selling flowers.

There was relief, but also a small ache. Relief because she was not lost. Ache because she was still a child, standing by a winter road with flowers in her hand.

In the end, both Tuzi and the couple bought flowers from her. They asked her to go back. It was not a grand rescue. It was a small roadside intervention: stop, ask, call for help, understand, buy the flowers, and hope she went home safely.

Later that night, Tuzi stayed near the Armenia border before heading toward Georgia customs the next day. The road was still moving forward, but this little winter scene stayed behind in the memory field.

This is one of those travel fragments that is difficult to classify. It is not sightseeing. It is not danger in the dramatic sense. It is a quiet human alarm: when something on the roadside looks too small, too alone, and too cold, you stop.
