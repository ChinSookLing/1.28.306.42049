Same Questions, Different Worlds

On 22/12/2023 and 23/12/2023, Tuzi asked Pakistani children the same simple questions she had once asked children in Waling, Nepal: What is your name? What do you want to be?

Real Story · 中文

同样的问题，孩子的答案却来自不同的世界。

在 Nepal 的 Waling，我曾经问孩子们几个很简单的问题： What is your name? What is your dream? What can you do to show you love Nepal? 那次的孩子们很会表达，英文也很自然，让我印象很深。

到了 Pakistan，我在 22/12/2023 于路边餐厅吃午餐。 那一带街上几乎看不到 women，所以作为一个 solo female overlander，我显得很特别。 很多人看着我，但那种眼神不是恶意，更多像是好奇：为什么这个外国女人会一个人开着 campervan 来到这里？

后来，有一个年轻男子会说一点 English，他主动来和我打招呼。 我就请他帮忙翻译，让我可以问附近的男孩子几个简单问题： What is your name? What do you want to be?

那些孩子的回答很真实。 他们的梦想常常跟自己每天看见的环境有关：driver、mechanic、shop owner。 这些答案不是小，也不是不好，而是说明孩子会从自己身边能看见的世界开始想象未来。

第二天，23/12/2023，我到了 Sukkur，遇到一个受教育程度比较好的家庭。 家里有几个 daughters。经过允许之后，我也问她们类似的问题。 她们的表达、confidence 和答案明显不同：有人想当 doctor，有人有更远的想象。

这让我很深地感觉到：孩子本身都很亮，可是 environment 和 education 会决定他们看见多远。 同样的问题，在不同环境里，会长出不同的答案。

Comparison Memory · Waling / Pakistan

The same questions travelled with Tuzi.

This Pakistan memory quietly connects back to the Waling children in Nepal. In Waling, the children answered with fluent English and strong confidence. In Pakistan, Tuzi saw two different layers within two days: roadside boys whose dreams reflected the world immediately around them, and Sukkur daughters whose education gave them wider language and wider futures.

The point is not to judge the children. The point is to see how environment, schooling, family support, and visible role models shape what a child can imagine.

English Memory Summary

Environment shapes imagination.

On 22/12/2023, Tuzi had lunch at a roadside restaurant in Pakistan. As a solo woman in a space where women were not visible on the street, she stood out. People stared, but without bad intention. With help from a young man who spoke some English, she interviewed local boys using the same simple questions she had once asked children in Waling, Nepal.

On 23/12/2023, in Sukkur, she met an educated family with daughters and asked similar questions again. The contrast was powerful: the roadside boys’ answers reflected their immediate environment, while the educated daughters spoke with wider confidence and dreams.

Heart Statement

孩子不是没有梦，是他们看见的世界不一样。

我问的是同样的问题。 可是答案告诉我：education、family、environment，会决定一个孩子可以把未来想多远。

The children were not different in worth. They were living inside different windows of possibility.
