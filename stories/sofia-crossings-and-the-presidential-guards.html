Sofia Crossings and the Presidential Guards

On 26 March 2024, Tuzi walked through central Sofia from 1:20pm to 7:20pm, reaching 15,869 steps and learning a new city rule: first observe how locals cross.

Real Story · 中文

Sofia 不是只看建筑，也要先学会怎么过马路。

26 March 2024，我在 Sofia 总统办公中心一带走路。 从下午 1:20pm 走到 7:20pm，一共 15,869 steps。 这一天很累，但也很值得。

在 Bulgaria，我开始学到一个以前不太熟悉的城市习惯： 过马路前，要先看当地人是怎么过的。 有些地方不是直接从路面过，而是走 underground passage。

Malaysia 和前面很多我开过的国家，很少有这种地下通道经验。 所以第一次我直接过马路时，被 driver scold 了。 我没有生气，因为这真的是 out of my knowledge。

Pedestrian Lesson

站太靠路边，也会让司机误会。

从那之后，我就会站在比较 inside 的位置，先观察 pedestrian 怎么过路， 然后才 follow them。

但也要记得，不能站得太靠近 roadside。 因为有些 driver 会以为你要过马路，然后停下来让你。 Road culture 每个国家都不一样，不懂的时候，先观察，比逞强重要。

Presidential Office

刚好碰上总统府换兵仪式。

那天我也刚好碰上 Bulgaria 总统府换兵仪式。 这种东西不是我特别计划到的， 可是在城市走路时，就这样自然遇见了。

走到最后，我在 Boris Park overnight。 Sofia 的一天变成了：15,869 steps、地下通道 lesson、 总统府换兵仪式，还有一个很累但很完整的城市记忆。

English Memory Summary

A city walk that became a lesson in local crossing rules.

On 26 March 2024, Tuzi walked through central Sofia from 1:20pm to 7:20pm, reaching 15,869 steps. The walk took her through the presidential office area and the city centre.

Sofia also taught her a practical lesson: before crossing roads in a new country, observe whether locals cross at street level or use underground passages. After being scolded once by a driver, Tuzi understood that this was not anger-worthy — it was simply knowledge she did not yet have.

Later, she happened to see the changing of the guards at the Bulgarian Presidential Office. That night, she overnighted at Boris Park, carrying both the tiredness of 15,869 steps and a new understanding of city road culture.

Heart Statement

有些城市不是先教你景点，而是先教你怎么安全地走过去。

Sofia taught me to watch the locals first: where they cross, where they wait, and when the city expects you to go underground.
