Iran · Chabahar

Three Hidden Wonders Around Chabahar

During the Iran family chapter, local friends brought Tuzi to places around Chabahar that few outsiders would normally reach.

Chabahar Pozm Tiyb Ramin Beach Tang Mud Volcano Local Friends Hidden Places

Location: Chabahar region, Iran

Context: During the Iran Family chapter

Note: This page records memory and gratitude. It is not a route guide or encouragement to repeat the journey without local support.

Real Story · 中文

有些地方，不是地图带你去，是人带你去。

在 Chabahar 那段时间，local friends 带我去了几个很少 outsider 会去的地方。 不是因为它们不美，而是因为 language barrier、sanctions、transport、network、local knowledge， 让很多外来 travellers 很难自己走进去。

这些地方对我来说不是普通景点。 它们是 Iran family chapter 的延伸：因为有人信任我、照顾我、带我出去， 我才看到这些 landscape。

所以这一页不是 travel guide。 这是 memory archive：记录 local friends 怎样把一个陌生 traveller 带进 Chabahar 更深的地方。

Hidden Wonder 01

Pozm Tiyb Mountain Beach.

Pozm Tiyb Mountain Beach felt like a place where sea and rock spoke loudly to each other. There were sandy plains, rocky formations, sea arches, wave sounds, seaweed, kelp, and the strange drum-like echo of water meeting stone.

This was not the polished side of tourism. It felt raw, coastal, and alive.

Hidden Wonder 02

Ramin Beach Chronicles.

Ramin Beach was another side of Chabahar: wind, sand, desert near the shore, and the feeling of walking into a landscape that did not behave like a normal beach.

With local guidance, the place became more than scenery. It became a memory of movement, laughter, sand, and the surprise of luminous plankton in the sea waves.

Hidden Wonder 03

Tang Mud Volcano.

Tang Mud Volcano showed a very different kind of wonder. It was not sea, not garden, not city. It was earth itself moving and breathing slowly — bubbling mud, strange ground, and a landscape shaped by pressure below the surface.

For Tuzi, this was another reminder that Chabahar was not one single place. It was coast, desert, mountain, village, family, wind, and geology all folded together.

English Memory Summary

Local places, local trust.

During Tuzi’s stay with her Iran family in Chabahar, local friends brought her to three rarely reached places: Pozm Tiyb Mountain Beach, Ramin Beach, and Tang Mud Volcano.

These trips were not simply sightseeing. They showed how local trust can open parts of a country that sanctions, language barriers, and logistics often keep invisible to outsiders.

Heart Statement

有些隐藏的地方，不是因为它们远，而是因为你需要有人带你进去。

Chabahar did not open through maps alone. It opened through people who said, “Come, I will show you.”
